Tips and Tricks on Updating Android 11 to 12 on Samsung Smartphones

Upgrade Samsung to Android 12 Step 1: Backup Your Data Before updating your Android device to a new version of the operating system, it is important to make sure you have backed up all your data. This includes any photos, videos, music files, documents, contact information, or other types of data stored on your phone. The easiest way to do this is to create a backup of your data on Google Drive, which can be done by going to the Settings section of your Samsung device and selecting the option to back up your data.

Step 2: Check Device Compatibility Before proceeding with the update, you should ensure your device is compatible with Android 12. To do this, go to the About Phone section of your device’s Settings menu and look for the Android Version. If your device is running an older version of Android (prior to Android 11), it may not be compatible with the new update.

Step 3: Download the Update Once you have verified that your device is compatible, the next step is to download the update. To do this, open the Samsung Software Update app on your device and select the option to download the update. Make sure you have a strong internet connection and enough storage space available on your device before beginning the download.

Step 4: Install the Update After the download has finished, it’s time to install the update. To do this, open the Samsung Software Update app and select the option to install the update. The installation process may take a few minutes, so make sure you have a full battery and are connected to a reliable source of power.

Step 5: Verify the Installation Once the installation is finished, it’s important to verify that the update was properly installed. To do this, open the Settings menu on your device and go to the About Phone section. Here, you should be able to find the Android version of your device, which should indicate that you have successfully updated to Android 12.

Additionally, make sure that all the apps and services on your device are up to date. Second, perform a factory reset of your device before updating to Android 12. This will ensure that all the problematic apps and services are removed and won’t interfere with the update process. Make sure to back up all your files and data first since the reset will erase everything on your device.

Third, make sure to disable any third-party applications that may conflict with the update process. These applications may interfere with the update process and cause the update to fail, so it’s best to disable them. Additionally, disable any “overclocking” or optimization of your device as this could also interfere with the update.

Fourth, make sure your device is connected to a secure and stable Wi-Fi connection when updating to Android 12. Updating your device over a slow or unstable connection can cause the update to fail or take a long time to complete. Therefore, it is best to make sure your device is connected to a strong and secure connection before beginning the update.

Finally, turn off automatic updates and ensure that your device has the latest version of Android 12 installed. This will ensure that your device is always up to date with the latest version and will prevent any potential issues that could arise from running an outdated version of Android.

By following these steps, you can ensure that your Samsung device is safe and secure when updating from Android 11 to Android 12. Securely backup your data, factory reset your device, disable any third-party applications, secure a stable Wi-Fi connection, and disable automatic updates to maintain stability while updating.

2. Preparation before the update#

Before the update, it is also important to free up some space on your device. You should delete unwanted apps, heavy files and media that you no longer need. Make sure there is minimum 8 GB empty space on your device, as this is enough for the update to install smoothly. If you need extra storage, you can transfer some of your videos, photos, or files to an external drive such as Google Drive, or simply delete them. Additionally, check your battery to make sure it is not too low when you update your device. If your battery is below 50%, it’s best to plug in your device and wait until it’s charged to at least 50%. Finally, make sure your Samsung device is connected to a good and stable Wi-Fi network prior to the update. A poor connection can cause your device to restart or freeze in the middle of the update process.

After backing up your data, ensure that the battery on your device is fully charged. This step is important to prevent your device from restarting or draining battery life during the update. When you know that the battery is fully charged, you can move on to the next step.

If you are using a Wi-Fi connection, you should also ensure that the connection is stable and fast enough to download the update. To do this, open the settings and look for the Wi-Fi network connection. If the connection is weak or slow, consider switching to a different Wi-Fi network.
