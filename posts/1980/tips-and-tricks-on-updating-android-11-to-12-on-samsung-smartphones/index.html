Step by Step Guide to Upgrading Android 11 to 12 on Samsung Devices

Upgrade Samsung to Android 12 Updating your Samsung smartphone from Android 11 to 12 can be intimidating, especially if you are unfamiliar with the process. Follow this step-by-step guide to learn how to upgrade your Android version successfully.

  1. Before you upgrade, backup your important files and data. It is important to save your files to an external source or cloud storage before starting the update process.

  2. Verify if your Samsung smartphone is compatible with the new version of Android. You can check on Google’s official website or the websites of your device’s manufacturer, like Samsung.

  3. Download the latest version of the Android OS. Be sure that you are downloading the most recent version available.

  4. Install the update on your Samsung smartphone. You may be able to install the new version of Android through an automated process (OTA update) or manually. If you choose to install manually, you will be required to select the appropriate file.

  5. Restart your device after the installation is complete. Your device will reboot to complete the installation process.

  6. Check to be sure that your device is running the latest version of Android. To do this, go to Settings > About Phone and check the version number.

These are the steps necessary to update your Samsung smartphone from Android 11 to 12. Ensure that you backup your important files prior to starting the process to prevent any data loss.

1. What’s New in Android 12#

Before you get started with upgrading your Android 11 to 12, it’s important to backup your files. This can be done by connecting your Samsung smartphone to a laptop or computer and simply copying the files from the device to the laptop or computer. Alternatively, you can also opt for internal storage tools such as Google Photos, Google Drive, and third-party cloud solutions. Once you have backed up your data, make sure to verify that it is indeed stored somewhere and is safe.

Next, ensure that your device has enough battery before you begin the upgrading process. An optimal battery level to start the update should be at least 70%. If it is anything below that, plug in your device to a charger and wait until your battery is fully charged before you start.

Now to begin the upgrade process, go to Settings in your Samsung smartphone. Then, scroll down and select Software Update (or ‘System Update’). Next, you will be prompted to check for updates. Select the ‘Check for Updates’ button and the device will begin looking for any available updates. If an upgrade is available, the prompt will show the Android version 12. It will also display the size of the upgrade and the estimated download time.

Once you have selected the upgrade button, the device will begin downloading the files needed to upgrade. It is advisable to keep your device connected to a reliable Wi-Fi network while the upgrade is in progress. Also, avoid using the device while the download is in progress as it could cause the upgrade process to fail.

Once the download process is complete, you will be prompted to install the update. Select the ‘Install Now’ button and the device will begin installing Android 12. After installation is complete, the device will restart and you will be presented with the new user-interface of the Android 12.

That’s it, you have now upgraded your Samsung smartphone successfully from Android 11 to 12. Make sure to go through the new features and settings to get the most out of the new operating system.

2. Preparation Before Upgrading#

It is important to remember to backup all important files before attempting any kind of upgrade. This will help make sure important data like music, pictures, videos, and documents will stay safe in case of any errors during the upgrade process. To access the Backup and Restore options, simply go to Settings and find the Backup and Reset tab. Here, tap on the Backup my Data option and follow the instructions on the screen. It is also advisable to backup to an external storage device. Once backed up, you are ready to upgrade your Android version from 11 to 12.

3. Upgrading Android 11 to 12 on Samsung Smartphones#

It is highly recommended to make a backup of important files or data stored on your device before proceeding with an upgrade to Android 12. This will ensure that all of your files and data remains safe and accessible after the upgrade process is complete. Additionally, make sure that your device has enough power to successfully complete the upgrade process. You should also check if your device has sufficient storage capacity and battery power. If not, you may be prompted for additional requirements before beginning the installation process.

Once these checks are complete, you can begin the updating process. First, open Settings on your device. Then select System, followed by About Phone. From there, press Software Update and check for the available Android 12 updates. If the update is available, click on the ‘install’ button.

The device will then begin the installation process. During this process, make sure to keep your device on the charger and that there is an adequate Wi-Fi connection available. It is also recommended to keep the device plugged in until the upgrade process is completed. Additionally, do not power off or restart the device during the upgrade.

During the installation process, the device may reboot several times and the progress bar should be monitored closely. Once the progress bar reaches 100%, the Android 12 download and installation is complete. After the device has restarted, the user should check that all settings and data are present and that no errors have occurred.

Finally, once all steps are complete, the user is ready to enjoy the new features that have been included with the Android 12 upgrade. Congratulations!

4. Troubleshooting Tips for Upgrading Android 11 to 12#

  1. Make sure your Samsung smartphone is fully charged or connected to a power supply before attempting the update.
  2. The Samsung Smart Switch app will help facilitate the update from Android 11 to 12. If the app is not already installed, head to the Google Play Store to install the app.
  3. Before upgrading, make sure to backup any important files you don’t want to lose.
  4. Double check that the app is up to date and all necessary drivers are installed.
  5. Once the update is complete, check to make sure your phone is running the correct version.
  6. Verify that the data stored on your device is still intact after the update.
