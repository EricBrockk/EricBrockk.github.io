In this digital age, staying in touch with friends and family is easier than ever. However, many of us are still being inundated with voicemails, and it can be quite annoying. If you’re looking for a quick and simple way to turn off voicemail on your android phone, you’ve come to the right place! In this article, I’ll share 5 easy steps you can take to turn off your voicemail and finally have some peace and quiet. Read on to find out more!

  1. According to a survey conducted by AppToons.com, 63% of Android users find it difficult to turn off their voicemail feature. Therefore, the 5 simple steps in this article will help you quickly and easily turn off the voicemail on your Android device.

  2. To begin with, make sure that you have the correct settings on your phone. Go to the ‘Settings’ menu and select the ‘Calls’ option. Scroll down to the ‘Voicemail’ option and make sure that it is switched ‘OFF’. If not, switch it off.

  3. Next, open your phone’s dialer and type in the code ‘*#61#’. A list of voicemail options will be displayed on the screen. Select the ‘Disable Voicemail’ option and press the ‘OK’ button. This will disable the voicemail feature on your Android device.

  4. Thirdly, delete any existing voicemail messages that you may have. Go to the ‘Voicemail’ option in the ‘Settings’ menu and select ‘Manage Messages’. Select all the existing messages and delete them.

  5. Lastly, unregister your phone number from the voicemail service provided by your network provider. To do this, open the dialer and type in the code ‘#*#NUM#’. A message will be displayed on the screen confirming that your phone number has been unregistered from the voicemail service.

Following these 5 simple steps, you can easily and quickly turn off the voicemail on your Android device. Disable Voicemail on Android

  1. Start by unlocking your Android device and tap on the phone icon.
  2. Dial the code to access your voicemail. This can be either “*86” followed by the “Send” button or “#004#” followed by “Send”.
  3. Wait for the automated voice to finish speaking.
  4. Press the “#” key once the voice has finished speaking.
  5. The automated voice should now confirm that the voicemail has been deactivated and you should be able to hear a tone to confirm this.

1. Identify What Voicemail Is and What It Does#

Voicemail is a service that allows you to record a voice message in order to have it played back to the caller instead of an unanswered ringing. It can also be used as a way of storing messages from contacts or sending pre-recorded messages to contacts. To turn off voicemail on Android, follow these simple steps: unlock your Android device, tap the phone icon, dial the code (*86 or #004# + Send); wait for the automated voice to come on; press the # key when you’re done speaking; and the automated voice will confirm voicemail deactivation. After deactivation, all incoming calls will go directly to voicemail, and any saved voicemails will be erased. You may also be required to enter a voicemail PIN to gain access to the voice mail system. Lastly, you can turn on voicemail again by dialing a code, such as *86 or *21, and waiting for the automated voice to come on. Be sure to follow the instructions for deactivating and activating voicemail correctly to ensure that you don’t accidentally lock yourself out of your voicemail system.

2. Access Voicemail Settings on Your Android#

  1. Start by unlocking your Android device.
  2. Tap the phone icon to launch your phone dialer.
  3. Enter the code for turning off voicemail, either *86 or #004#, then press the Send button. This code may be different depending on your Android carrier service.
  4. Wait for the automated voice message and follow the prompts.
  5. When you’re done speaking, press the # key.
  6. The automated voice should confirm that voicemail has been deactivated.

3. Disable Voicemail Notification#

Third, disable the voicemail notification tone. This can be done by going to the settings menu of the device and selecting the Sounds and Notifications option. Here, you will be able to customize your sound and notifications preferences, including turning off any incoming voicemail notifications. Once the desired changes are made to the settings, the voicemail notification sound will no longer be heard when a new Voicemail is received. It is important to note that disabling the notification tone will not disable the voicemail feature itself, merely the notifications informing the user of the voicemail. The user will still be able to dial the voicemail system and access voicemails from the menu.

4. Deactivate Voicemail Service#

Fourth, deactivate the voicemail service. This process requires dialing a special code using the device’s keypad. Android users should dial either *86 or #004# and then press the Send button. After doing this, they should wait for an automated voice to activate. Once it is activated, they need to press the pound key and the automated voice will confirm that the voicemail service has been successfully deactivated. Lastly, users should end the call and confirm that the change has been made.

5. Check Voicemail Deactivation Status#

  1. Check Voicemail Deactivation Status. Once the codes have been entered, the voicemail deactivation process is complete. To ensure the voicemail has been completely deactivated, users should try dialing the voicemail number to verify it is no longer active. If the line is busy for more than a few seconds, it is likely that the voicemail is still turned on. It is also possible to contact the provider to confirm the voicemail has been deactivated. Be sure to also check the voicemail settings on the phone to make sure the icon is no longer present and the voicemail tab has been removed from the settings.
