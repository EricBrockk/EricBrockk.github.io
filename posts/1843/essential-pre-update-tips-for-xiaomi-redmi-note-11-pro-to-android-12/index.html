Step by Step Guide to Updating Xiaomi Redmi Note 11 Pro to Android 12

  1. Back Up Your Data: Before you start any update for your Xiaomi Redmi Note 11 Pro, be sure to back up all of your important data such as photos, videos, contacts, and other files. You can easily back up your data to the cloud, an external hard drive, or a USB drive.

  2. Update Your Apps: Make sure that all the apps you have installed on your Xiaomi Redmi Note 11 Pro are updated to the latest versions. This will ensure that the apps are optimized for the new Android version and will work properly after the update.

  4. Clear Cache: Before starting the update, clear all caches and temporary files. This will free up disk space and help the update run smoothly.

  5. Charge Your Phone: Make sure your Xiaomi Redmi Note 11 Pro is fully charged before you start the update. This will ensure that the update process doesn’t get interrupted due to a low battery.

  6. Disconnect from Wi-Fi and Bluetooth: Before you start the update, make sure to disconnect from any Wi-Fi and Bluetooth connections. This will prevent any connection issues during the update process.

  7. Install the Update: Finally, after all of the preparations, you can install the update for your Xiaomi Redmi Note 11 Pro to the Android 12 version. 1. Check Battery Capacity#

It’s also essential to ensure your phone’s battery is fully charged before beginning the update. Weak battery power can cause an interruption during the update and potentially damage your device. Ensure your battery has at least 80% charged before beginning, and it’s recommended to keep the phone connected to a power source during the update. Additionally, you should also close any background apps to reduce the load on your device’s processor and ensure the update proceeds as quickly and smoothly as possible.

2. Back Up Data#

It is also necessary to clean up the phone before updating. Uninstalling unnecessary apps, clearing cache from all apps, and deleting duplicate files are some steps that will free up some space and improve the performance in the long run. Additionally, make sure to back up the important data such as text messages, music and contacts. This can be done through a desktop application or an external hard drive. It is also wise to back up the data to cloud storage as an added precaution. Finally, make sure to update all the apps on the phone to their latest version before initiating the update to Android 12.

3. Free Up Storage Space#

It’s also important to free up storage space on your device before updating. Make sure to delete any unwanted or unused apps or files to create more space. Android 12 takes up more storage than previous versions, so try to create a few GB of space to ensure the update runs smoothly. You can also move your existing apps, videos, and photos onto an external storage device like a cloud drive or a USB drive to further free up space. Don’t forget to back up any important information before deleting it.
