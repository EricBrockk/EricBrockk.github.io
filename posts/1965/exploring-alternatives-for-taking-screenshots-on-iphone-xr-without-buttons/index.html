Step By Step Guide to Taking Screenshots on iPhone XR Without Buttons

Take iPhone XR Screenshot

Understanding How to Take Screenshots on iPhone XR Without Buttons Objective: This tutorial will provide a guide to explore alternative methods for taking screenshots on an iPhone XR without physical button.

Steps:

  1. Go to Settings, hit General, and then tap on Accessibility.
  2. Select “AssistiveTouch” and turn it on.
  3. Upon turning AssistiveTouch on, a white circle with a grey box will appear on the screen.
  4. Tap the grey box and then hit “Device”.
  5. Under the Device section, tap “ More”
  6. Select “Screenshot” and tap “Done.”
  7. To take a screenshot, simply tap the white circle and select “Screenshot”. A preview of the screenshot will appear at the bottom of the screen.

Conclusion: The tutorial has provided an overview of how to take a screenshot on an iPhone XR without physical buttons. By turning on the AssistiveTouch feature, you now have an alternative method to take a screenshot.

1. Overview of the iPhone XR’s Physical Design#

The iPhone XR has a unique physical design that distinguishes it from other models in the iPhone lineup; it does not have a home button, and instead relies on a series of swiping motions to navigate the device. The device features an edge-to-edge display that covers the entire face of the phone, as well as a notch at the top that houses the front-facing camera and other sensors. While this makes the device easier to use and navigate, it also presents a problem when trying to take screenshots. The traditional method of taking screenshots—by pressing the home button and power button—is not available on the iPhone XR. Fortunately, there are alternatives for taking screenshots on the iPhone XR, which will be explored in this article.

The first alternative to taking screenshots on the iPhone XR is to use the AssistiveTouch feature. This feature is found in the Settings menu, under General and then Accessibility. When AssistiveTouch is enabled, an icon appears on the screen that acts as a virtual home button. This virtual button can be used to access the menu which allows users to take screenshots. To take screenshots with AssistiveTouch, users have to select the “Device” option from the grey box that appears when the AssistiveTouch icon is tapped. From there, users can select the “Take Screenshot” option and the screenshot will be saved to the camera roll.

Another alternative method for taking screenshots on the iPhone XR is to use the Apple Pencil. The Apple Pencil can be used to take screenshots by tapping and holding on the screen and then dragging the Pencil across the display. This will capture the image of whatever is currently on the screen and save it to the camera roll.

Finally, users can also take screenshots on the iPhone XR by using the Voice Control feature. This feature is also found in the Accessibility settings, and it allows users to control their phone with their voice. To take screenshots, users can say “Take a screenshot” and the device will automatically capture the image of whatever is currently on the screen and save it to the camera roll.

These are the available alternatives for taking screenshots on the iPhone XR without the use of the home button. By using the AssistiveTouch, Apple Pencil or Voice Control, users can still capture images of their device and share them with others.

2. Exploring Alternative Solutions#

  1. To take screenshots on your iPhone XR, you can also use your AssistiveTouch settings. To get to these settings, open your Settings app then select General. From the General menu, select Accessibility and then scroll down to AssistiveTouch.
  2. Once you’ve enabled AssistiveTouch, you’ll see a grey box appear at the bottom left of your screen. Tap this to open the AssistiveTouch menu.
  3. From the AssistiveTouch menu, select Device and then you’ll be presented with various options including Lock Screen, Home, and Screenshot.
  4. Select Screenshot and then you’ll be able to capture what’s currently on your iPhone XR’s screen. You’ll also receive a notification that the screenshot was successful.
  5. In the AssistiveTouch menu, you can also choose to take a screenshot of the selected area, allowing you to capture just a part of the screen.
  6. To take a screenshot of the selected area, select the Screenshot option then select Selected Area from the menu. You’ll then be able to select the area you’d like to capture.
