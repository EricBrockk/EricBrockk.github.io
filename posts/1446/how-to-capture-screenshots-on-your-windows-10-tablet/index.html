Step by Step Guide to Screenshot on a Windows 10 Tablet

Step by Step Screenshot Windows 10 Taking screenshots on your Windows 10 tablet isn’t as straightforward as you might expect. There is no dedicated button for this purpose, as there is on smartphones and some laptops. However, it is still possible to do this and here are the steps/instructions to take a screenshot on your Windows 10 tablet:

  1. Tap the Windows Key + Volume Down at the same time. This will take a screenshot of whatever is currently on your tablet’s screen.

  2. That screenshot will be saved to your tablet’s Pictures/Screenshots folder.

  3. To share that screenshot, use the Windows share tool: press the Windows+H keys.

  4. This will open up a menu of options for sharing the screenshot. You can choose apps like Mail, Facebook, Twitter, OneNote, and more.

  5. If you want to edit the screenshot, you can open it in Paint 3D or any other image editor.

  6. If you want to take a screenshot of just one app, you can press Alt+PrtScn. This will save the screenshot to the clipboard, where you can then choose to edit or share it.

These are the steps to take a screenshot on your Windows 10 tablet. With these simple instructions, you can easily capture what’s on your tablet’s screen.

1. How to take Screenshots on a Windows 10 Tablet#

To capture a screenshot on your Windows 10 tablet, press and hold the Windows Key and the Volume Down key at the same time. The tablet will take a screenshot of your whole screen, and you will be able to view the screenshot in the Photos app. If you’re running Windows 10 May 2019 Update or newer, you can also access your screenshots through the Snip & Sketch app. To access Snip & Sketch, search for the app in the Start menu or open it from the notification area and select the ‘New’ button. You can also open the app from the Windows + Shift + S keys. If you need to edit your screenshots, you can use the Paint 3D app to crop and rotate the image. Finally, you can export the screenshot to a different file format, such as JPG, or share the image quickly using the Windows share menu.

2. What Tools are available to Capture Screenshots#

There are a number of built-in tools available on Windows 10 that help users capture screenshots. The Windows Key + Volume Down command is an easy way to take a full screenshot without the need to install any additional tools. Additionally, the Snipping Tool has been available since Windows Vista, allowing users to capture a specific area of their screen and annotate it. Windows 10 also has a built-in Game Bar that can be used to capture screenshots in game. Additionally, the Print Screen button can be used to capture your entire screen and automatically save it to the clipboard. All these tools make it easy to capture screenshots quickly and easily on any Windows 10 tablet.

3. How to Easily Save and Share Your Screenshots#

Once you have taken your screenshot, you will find it in the Photos app where you will be able to view, edit, and share your image quickly and easily. To save your screenshot, simply click the three dots in the top right corner of the photo to reveal the menu. Select Save as from the menu and the screenshot will be saved to your device. You can find it from the File Explorer app.

If you would like to share your screenshot with someone, click the three dots again and select “Share” from the menu. You will then be given the option to share your screenshot using your preferred method such as email, messaging, and social media.

Furthermore, you can also copy the screenshot to the clipboard by clicking the “Copy” button. This will allow you to paste the screenshot into any text editor or other application that supports pasting from the clipboard.

Additionally, if you want to rename the screenshot, click the three dots again and select “ Rename” from the menu. This will allow you to edit the filename before you save it which can help you to keep your screenshots organised.

Finally, if you would like to delete the screenshot, click the three dots again and select “Delete” from the menu. This will remove the screenshot from the Photos app as well as from your device’s memory.

4. Tips to Improve Your Screenshot Experience#

  1. Improve the accuracy of your screenshot by using the Windows Snip Tool. This tool enables you to select any area of the screen to capture and save.

  2. To capture a quick screenshot on your Windows 10 tablet, press the Windows Key + Volume Down at the same time. This will take a screenshot of the whole screen.

  3. To capture a screenshot of only one window, press Alt + Print Screen. This will take a screenshot of the window currently open and save it as a file.

  4. To capture a screenshot of only a portion of the screen, press Windows Key + Shift + S. This will display a menu with options for rectangle, free-form or window snip.

  5. To add quick annotations to your screenshot, press Windows Key + Shift + A. This will display a menu with options for adding lines, circles, arrows and text.

  6. To add more detailed annotations to your screenshot, use the Windows Snip Tool again. This tool has more options and you can also save the annotated screenshot to a file.

Q: How do I take a screenshot on my Windows 10 tablet? A: To take a screenshot on a Windows 10 tablet, press and hold the Windows logo button and the Volume down button at the same time. Your tablet screen will dim briefly, then the screenshot will save in the Screenshots folder of your picture library.

Q: How can I find my saved screenshots? A: Your saved screenshots will appear in a folder named “Screenshots” in the Pictures library of your computer. The folder can also be accessed by clicking on the Photos app on the Start Menu.

Q: What file formats do the screenshots save as? A: Screenshots taken on a Windows 10 tablet are saved as PNG files.

Q: Is there a shortcut key for taking screenshots? A: Yes, you can quickly take a screenshot by pressing the Windows key + PrtScn at the same time.

Q: Can I customize the screenshot settings? A: Yes, you can customize the screenshot settings in the settings of your Windows 10 tablet. Go to Settings > Ease of Access > Keyboard and turn on the “Use the PrtScn button to open screen snipping” option.
