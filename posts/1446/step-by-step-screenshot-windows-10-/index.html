1. Overview of Taking Screenshots#

Taking screenshots can be an incredibly helpful tool for capturing important images on your tablet or computer. Screenshots are especially valuable when you need to save or share information quickly, such as when sharing images of error messages or other technical issues. Here’s a step-by-step guide to taking screenshots on Windows 10 tablets.

To take a screenshot of an app, press and hold the Windows logo and volume down button on your tablet simultaneously. You should hear a shutter sound, which indicates that the screenshot has been taken. The screenshot will be saved to the Pictures > Screenshots folder, which can be found on your tablet.

To take a screenshot of your entire desktop, press and hold the Windows logo and the PrtScn (or Print Screen) button. Again, you should hear the shutter sound, indicating that the screenshot has been taken. You can then open the screenshot from the Pictures > Screenshots folder.

If you need to take a screenshot of a specific portion of your desktop, press and hold the Windows logo, Shift, and the S keys simultaneously. Your screen should dim briefly, indicating that you can now select the area you wish to screenshot. Once you have done this, click the mouse and the screenshot will be taken.

In order to check the screenshot you have taken, navigate to the Pictures > Screenshots folder and you should see the image you captured. You can now open the screenshot, save it, or share it with others.

Once you have taken the screenshot, you can edit it by using Paint 3D, a free application that comes pre-installed on Windows 10. From the Home screen of Paint 3D, select Open and find the screenshot. You can now use the drawing and editing tools to adjust the screenshot as needed.

Finally, if you need to delete a screenshot, locate the image in the Pictures > Screenshots folder and delete it. You can also delete multiple screenshots by selecting them and using the Delete key.

With these simple steps, taking and editing screenshots on your Windows 10 tablet should be a breeze.

2. Preparing the Device#

Once you have found the app you wish to take a screenshot of, press and hold both the Windows logo and volume down buttons at the same time. Once you have done this, the device will produce a shutter sound, indicating the screenshot has been taken. Now, check the screenshot in the Screenshots folder within your device’s Photos app or Pictures library to make sure the image is what you wished to capture. If you have a device with a USB port, you can also transfer the screenshots to a computer for further editing. To do so, simply drag and drop the screenshots from the device into the Pictures library or equivalent file explorer on the computer.

3. Capture Screenshots#

After taking the screenshot, it is saved to the Screenshots folder in the Pictures library of your tablet. To access the screenshot, open the Pictures library, and then select the Screenshots folder. You can view, share, rename, or delete the screenshot from this folder.

Another way to access the screenshot is to open the Notification Panel and select the Screenshot Captured notification. This will take you directly to the screenshot.

Furthermore, you can also edit the screenshot. To do so, open the screenshot from either the Screenshots folder or the Notification Panel, and then select Edit from the menu. This will open the photo in the Photos app where you can crop, rotate, or add effects to the screenshot.

You can also take a screenshot within an app. To do so, press and hold the Power button then select the Screenshot option. The screenshot will be captured, and saved to the Screenshots folder in the Pictures library.

Additionally, you can also take screenshots of specific windows. To do so, press and hold the Alt key, then press the Print Screen key. The screenshot of the active window will be captured, and saved to the Screenshots folder.

Finally, you can also add annotations to the screenshot. To do so, open the screenshot from either the Screenshots folder or the Notification Panel, and then select Draw from the menu. This will open the photo in the Photos app where you can draw, or add text to the screenshot.

4. Save the Screenshot#

You can now find your screenshot in the Pictures folder. You can also find the screenshot in the notification center, located at the top-right corner of your tablet screen. Simply select the notification to open the screenshot in the Photos app for cropping, sharing or editing. If you’ve connected your tablet to your Microsoft account, you can also find all your screenshots in the cloud. Lastly, you could print the screenshot or send it as an email attachment.

Q. What exactly is a screenshot? A. A screenshot is an image of the current state of your computer screen. It allows you to capture a moment in time and share it with others.

Q. How do you take a screenshot on a Windows 10 tablet? A. Taking a screenshot on a Windows 10 tablet is fairly easy. First, essentially press the Windows logo and Volume Down button at the same time. The screen should dim momentarily and the capture should be saved to the Screenshots folder in the Pictures library.

Q. What if I don’t have a Windows logo button? A. You can also take a screenshot by pressing the Fn+Windows logo+Space bar keys. This should also capture the current screen.

Q. Can I take a screenshot of only a specific area of my screen? A. Yes, you can take a screenshot of only a specific area. To do this, press the Windows logo and the Shift+Volume Down buttons at the same time. Place the box over the desired area and then use the mouse or stylus to adjust the size and placement of the box.

Q. How do I access and view the screenshots I have taken? A. All screenshots are stored in the Screenshots folder which can be found in the Pictures library. You can access the Pictures library by going to This PC > Pictures. From there, you should be able to view and access any screenshots you have taken.
