1. Understand the process of transferring photos from Android to iPhone#

To start the photo transfer process from Android to iPhone, first you should ensure that both devices are connected to the same Wi-Fi network. Then, open Google Photos on your Android and turn on Back Up & Sync. After that, you can select DCIM for backup and this will allow you to transfer to any type of device. When the backup is finished, then you can open Google Photos on your iPhone and tap the menu icon. Afterwards, select Device Folders and your photos will automatically be transferred from Android to iPhone.

2. Connect your iPhone to your computer#

Once the iPhone setup is complete, make sure both your devices are connected to the same Wi-Fi network. On your Android device, open the Google Photos app, go to Settings>Back up & sync and then toggle it on if it hasn’t been done already. Next, you’ll want to select which photos you’d like to back up and sync including the DCIM folder. Now, on your iPhone, open the Google Photos app, select the ‘Device Folders’ tab at the bottom of the screen, and then choose the folder named ‘DCIM’ to restore your Android photos to the iPhone. Once selected, just choose the photos you wish to transfer and you’re good to go. Alternatively you can also use a USB cable and connect your Android to your computer, sync and transfer photos that way as well.

3. Install the Android File Transfer app#

  1. After you have successfully set up both of your devices, go to the App Store on your iPhone and download the Android File Transfer app. This app will allow your iPhone to access your Android device.
  2. Then, connect both of your devices to the same Wi-Fi network.
  3. Install the Android File Transfer app and open it.
  4. Now, open the Google Photos app on your Android device and turn on the Back Up & Sync option. This will ensure your photos are backed up on the cloud.
  5. Once the photos are backed up in the cloud, you can easily access them from your iPhone. Select the DCIM folder and all of your photos will be transferred.
  6. You will find all of your photos in the Android File Transfer folder on your iPhone.

4. Copy the photos from Android to iPhone#

  1. Before starting the transfer, make sure both your Android and iPhone device are connected to the same Wi-Fi network.
  2. Open the Google Photos app on your Android phone, then turn on the Back Up & Sync option.
  3. Choose where to store the backup of your photos, and then select the DCIM folder.
  4. After doing that, your Android phone will automatically sync your photos with the Google Photos app.
  5. Now on the iPhone, open the Google Photos app and sign in with the same Google Account you used to back up your photos on Android.
  6. All the photos from your Android phone will now be automatically transferred to the Google Photos app on your iPhone.
