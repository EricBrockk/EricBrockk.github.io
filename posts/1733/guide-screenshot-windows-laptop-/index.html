How to Take Screenshots on Windows Surface Laptop

  6. The screenshot will automatically be saved in the “Screenshots” folder under the “Pictures” folder.

  7. To take a screenshot of just a certain area of your Windows Surface Laptop, press and hold down the Windows logo and press the Volume Up button at the same time.

  8. You will now see a cross hair on the screen. Use your mouse or tablet pen to drag the area you want to capture.

  9. The screenshot will automatically be saved in the “Screenshots” folder under the “Pictures” folder.

1. Overview of Windows Surface Laptop#

The Windows Surface Laptop running Windows 10 is a high-performance laptop with a touchscreen interface, and it has lots of features. Taking screenshots with a Windows Surface Laptop is easy and fast. All you need to do is press and hold the Windows logo and Volume Down button at the same time. This will generate a brief animation, and the screen will dim, indicating that the screenshot has been taken. The image will be automatically saved to your device.

To make sure that you get the perfect screenshot, you can also use the Snipping Tool. This allows you to quickly capture a specific area of the screen with the click of a button. It also allows you to edit your screenshot before saving it to your device.

If you are using the latest version of Windows 10 operating system, you can use the new Game Bar feature to take screenshots with ease. When you press the Windows logo and G key simultaneously, the Game Bar will open and you can click on the camera icon to take a screenshot.

You can also take screenshots with the Window Surface Pen. Just press and hold the top part of the pen and press the top button to take a screenshot. Any screenshot you take with the pen will be automatically saved to your device.

In addition to the screenshot options, you can also record video clips of your screen activity. You can use the same snipping tool to start recording and the Windows logo and G key to start the Game Bar and start recording.

If you want to share your screenshot or video with others, you can use the Windows Share feature to quickly upload it to social media, email, or other cloud storage services. It’s quick and easy to share with friends and family.

2. Ways To Take Screenshots on Windows Surface Laptop#

  1. You can edit and customize the way your screenshots are saved by pressing the Windows Key + PrtScn. Select the destination folder and the desired image file type for the screenshots in the Settings tab.

  2. You can take delayed screenshots using the Snipping Tool. This is available on the taskbar or you can type Snipping Tool in the search box.

  3. You can open the task manager by pressing the Ctrl + Shift + Esc buttons. Select the File tab and select Create dump file. This will create a screenshot of your current driver and processes.

  4. If you have an external monitor connected to your laptop, you can press the Windows Key + Shift + S to get a partial screenshot of the screen. The area where you initiate the command will be highlighted and you can freely move it around to your desired area.

  5. You can use the Print Screen key (PS) to snap a screenshot of the entire screen. You can also press the Fn + PS keys to take the screenshot of just the active window.

  6. For more advanced users, you can use a third-party program to take screenshots. Programs such as Lightshot and ShareX are great alternatives to the built-in screenshot functions on Windows. They offer more options to customize and edit your screenshots.

3. Further Tips on Taking Screenshots on Windows Surface Laptop#

  1. You can also take a screenshot by pressing the Print Screen key on your keyboard. This will save the image to your clipboard, where you can paste it into an image editor.

  2. To take a screenshot of a specific window, press Alt + Print Screen at the same time. This will capture exactly what is in the window, without any of the extra background.

  3. If you want to take a specific area of the screen, press Windows key + Shift + S. This will open up the snipping bar, which you can use to capture a selection of the screen.

  4. Alternatively, you can use the Snip & Sketch tool to take a full-screen or windows screenshot. Simply open the tool from the Start Menu, then use the mouse to select the desired area of the screen.

  5. You can also use a stylus to take a screenshot on your Windows Surface Laptop. Simply single-click the top button on the stylus to take a screenshot. This will open the screen snipping tool, where you can select the desired area of the screen.

  6. In Windows 10, you can also use the Game Bar to take a screenshot. Press the Windows logo and G at the same time to open the Game Bar. Then, click the ‘Take Screenshot’ button to take a screenshot of the active window, or use the ‘Full screen’ button to take a screenshot of your entire screen.

4. Conclusion#

The screenshot taken on a Windows Surface Laptop using this method is stored on the device you are using. It’s easy to find, as the screenshot will be saved in the Screenshots folder located in the Pictures Library. You can transfer the screenshot to other devices simply by copying the image from the Screenshots folder to any location on the device. You can also share the screenshot immediately via email, messaging, or other services that you have connected. This quick and easy guide will ensure you know how to take a screenshot on a Windows Surface Laptop with ease.

Q1: What is a screenshot? A1: A screenshot is a digital image of what is visible on a computer monitor, television, or other visual output device. It can also be referred to as a screen grab or screen capture.

Q2: How do I take a screenshot on a Windows Surface Laptop? A2: To take a screenshot on a Windows Surface Laptop, press and hold the Windows logo button and the Volume Down button at the same time for about 2 seconds. The screen will dim to indicate that a screenshot has been taken and saved.

Q3: Where can I find the screenshot? A3: The screenshot will be saved in the “Pictures” folder in the “Screenshots” folder. You can access the folder by navigating to the “Start” button > “File Explorer” > “Pictures” > “Screenshots”.

Q4: What file type is the screenshot? A4: Screenshots taken on the Windows Surface Laptop are usually saved as .PNG files.

Q5: Can I edit the screenshot? A5: Yes, you can easily edit the screenshot with a photo-editing software or app like Paint.net or Adobe Photoshop.
