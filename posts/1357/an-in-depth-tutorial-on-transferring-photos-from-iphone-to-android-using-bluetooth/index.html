For a successful transfer, begin by making sure both phones are visible. On your iPhone, enter the Bluetooth settings and turn on Bluetooth visibility. 4. Troubleshooting Tips#

If you encounter difficulty when transferring photos from your iPhone to your Android device via Bluetooth, you should first check to make sure that the devices are correctly paired. You may need to unpair and re-pair them or make sure that your Bluetooth is turned on.

It may seem obvious, but make sure that the photos you wish to transfer are actually in the album or folder you are trying to transfer from. Occasionally, photos can become misplaced within the phone’s gallery.

It is important that your iPhone’s Bluetooth and your Android’s Bluetooth have the same version. Otherwise, the phones won’t be able to communicate and the transfer won’t complete.

When you begin the transfer, check to make sure that the progress bar on both phones is moving. If it is not, then you may need to try restarting the process.

Another key factor that can affect the speed of transfer is the distance between the phones. Try to keep both phones around the same distance from one another while the transfer is taking place. Transferring over Bluetooth requires a good connection, so make sure the range between the phones is not too far.

Make sure you have the appropriate permissions set on both phones for Bluetooth transfers. On your iPhone, make sure that the Authorization to Transfer Data setting is enabled. On your Android, ensure your Bluetooth connections have permission to access your pictures and media.

Finally, if you are still having difficulty, you may need to reset your Bluetooth connection. Resetting your Bluetooth can help with any minor issues that could be preventing a successful transfer.
