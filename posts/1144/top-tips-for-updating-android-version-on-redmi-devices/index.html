1.Check for System Updates#

Firstly, open the Settings app and tap General Settings. Look for the About Phone/Device section and click on it. Your device’s current Android version and device model information will be displayed. Make sure that there is a software update available for your specific device model before proceeding. Once you have verified that you can update your phone’s Android version, take the time to back up all of your data and settings. This can be done by going to Settings > Backup & Reset and selecting the Back Up My Data and Automatic Restore options. You can also use any specialized backup apps to back up specific data and settings.

2.Create a Backup#

To ensure a smooth updating process, users should make sure to create a backup of their data and settings first. To check what version of Android your Redmi device is running, simply go to Settings, and tap About Phone/Device. After that, you can start backing up your contacts, files, and apps, and make sure to have enough storage space to install the new update. Don’t forget to connect your device to a reliable internet connection before starting the backup process. Once the process is complete, you can continue with the Android version update.

3.Download the Firmware#

In order to update your Redmi device’s Android version, you will need to download the latest version of the firmware compatible with your device. Make sure to download the exact version that you need, as downloading the wrong version can cause issues on your device. Downloading the firmware is easy, simply go to the website of your device manufacturer and search for the latest version of the firmware that is compatible with your device. Once you have downloaded the firmware, transfer it to your device via a USB cable or a memory card.

4.Install the System Update#

Once you have checked the current version, open the Settings menu again and select System Updates to start the update process. Ensure that your Redmi device is connected to a reliable Wi-Fi connection before starting the update. You will be prompted to download the update package, then tap Install to begin the installation process. Once the download and installation process is complete, your Redmi device will automatically restart to finish the update.

You can also choose to update your Redmi device manually. To do this, you will need to download the recovery ROM file from the official Xiaomi website and copy it to your Redmi device’s storage folder. Open your Redmi device’s Settings menu and select System Updates, then tap the three-dot menu and select Choose update package to select the recovery ROM file you have downloaded. Tap Install Now to begin installing the update. The installation process may take some time, but your Redmi device will reboot when it is done.

It is important to remember to check the System Updates menu periodically to ensure that your Redmi device is running the latest version of Android. This will help keep your device secure and will also ensure you have access to the latest features and apps. If there is an available update, make sure to backup your data and settings before starting.

Finally, make sure to keep your Redmi devices powered by using a compatible charger and USB cable. Updating your Redmi device’s Android version may require additional battery power, so make sure that you have your device fully charged before starting any updates.
