Step by Step Guide to Updating Android Version 9 to 12

Upgrade Android 9 to 12 If you are considering upgrading your Android device from version 9 to version 12, there are some important factors you should be aware of before you begin. Here is a brief guide of what you need to know before updating your Android version.

  1. Check Compatibility - Before beginning the upgrade process, you should check to make sure your device is compatible with the Android version 12 update. Not all Android devices will be able to run the latest software, and it is important to confirm that your device is capable before beginning.

  2. Backup Your Data - Before starting the upgrade process, make sure to backup your data. This means backing up all of your photos, contacts, files and other important information that you want to keep. This way, if something goes wrong, you can restore these items quickly and easily.

  3. Download the Update - Once you have confirmed your device is compatible and backed up your data, you can begin the downloading the update. This can usually be done through your device’s Settings menu. Once the download is complete, your device will be ready to upgrade.

  4. Update Your Apps - Once your device is ready to go, you should update any apps that you have installed. This is not necessary, but it is recommended as new software versions often come with bug fixes and new features.

  5. Check for Automated Updates - After you have performed the upgrade, you should check for automated updates. This is to ensure that your device remains up to date with the latest security patches and bug fixes. This can usually be done through the device’s Settings menu as well.

By following these steps, you can safely upgrade your Android device from version 9 to version 12. Remember to check for compatibility, back up your data, download the update, update your apps and check for automated updates. Doing this will ensure that your device is running the most recent version of Android smoothly and securely.

2. Confirm Your Android Device Is Compatible With Android 12#

Before you start the update process, you need to make sure that your device is compatible with Android 12. To do this, you can use your device’s built-in Compatibility Checker tool or look up its specs online. This will ensure that your device can handle Android 12’s new features and apps. You should also check for any compatibility issues with your existing apps before upgrading. If you find any, you may need to uninstall the old app and install the updated version. It’s also important to make sure you have enough free space and battery life on your device before attempting the update. Lastly, before initiating the update, back up all your important data such as contacts, photos, videos, music, and documents. This way, you will have a copy of your files in case something goes wrong during the update process.

3. Preparing Your Device and Backup Data#

Before updating your Android device, it’s important to prepare your device and back up important data. To ensure a smooth transition to the new version, make sure that your device is compatible with Android 12. You can check this by going to the System Settings on your device and checking which Android version is currently running on your device. It’s also important to back up all your data. This can be done through Google’s own Backup and Reset feature found in the System Settings of your device. Back up your contacts, photos, documents, and other content to the cloud. Make sure your device has sufficient storage space prior to updating. Lastly, ensure you have a reliable internet connection when updating your device.

4. Installing the Android 12 Update#

Before updating your device to the newest version, there are several things you must do. First, you must check your device’s system requirements to make sure it is compatible with the new Android version. Depending on your device manufacturer, a system update may be available, so you should check to see if such an update is available. You should also ensure you have around 500-800 megabytes of free space available, and determine if your device is rooted or not, as this may affect the update process.

Next, you should back up your device’s data as there is a chance that you may lose all of your data during the update process. Once the download is done, you can install the files and launch the setup utility. It will guide you through the installation process and show you the progress of the update. When that is done, you may need to restore your data and customize the settings for the new OS, after which you can start using the Android 12 upgrade.
