Before taking a screenshot on the Samsung A13 phone, make sure you have everything you want in the shot ready to go; otherwise, you’ll have to take multiple screenshots. To view and edit your screenshot, open the Gallery app, located on the home screen; in the top right, tap the overflow button. Additionally, users can toggle on the Palm Swipe Capture setting in the Advanced Features menu on their Samsung A13 phone, allowing them to take screenshots by simply swiping the side of their hand across the phone’s display.

3. Tips and Tricks for Optimizing the Screenshot Function on the Samsung A13 Phone#

  1. There are a few steps to take to easily take a screenshot with your Samsung A13 phone. Firstly, press and hold the Power and Volume Down buttons simultaneously until you hear a shutter sound from your device. This indicates that a screenshot has been taken and is now available in your phone’s Gallery app.

  2. If you want to customize and edit the screenshot, simply open the Gallery app and locate the screenshot. Tap the overflow button in the top right corner to open up various customization options including cropping, annotating, and drawing.

  3. You can also capture the entire page of a website or application by pressing and holding the Power button until a small menu appears. Select the “Scroll Capture” option from this menu, then tap the screen to take a screenshot of the full page.

  4. To further speed up the process, you can enable the screenshot quick launch feature. Open the Settings app and navigate to Advanced Features > Motions and Gestures, then toggle the Palm Swipe to Capture option. Now you can quickly take a screenshot by swiping the side of your hand across the display.

  5. You can also take a screenshot with your voice using the “Hi Bixby” feature. Simply say “Hi Bixby” followed by “Take a screenshot” and the phone will capture the screen and upload it to your Gallery app immediately.

  6. Finally, you can also use the Assistive Touch feature as a shortcut to take screenshots. Go to the Settings app and navigate to Accessibility > Dexterity and Interaction > Assistive Touch, then toggle the option to enable it. A small, translucent icon will appear on the screen that you can tap and hold to open a menu with a screenshot option.

4. Conclusion#

When you no longer need a screenshot, you can delete it from the Gallery app. To do this, tap on the image thumbnail and select the ‘Delete’ button. You can also share screenshots directly from the Gallery app by tapping the share button. Additionally, you can make use of the Samsung Duplicate feature which allows you to copy an image from the Gallery app and insert it as a new image. This feature is great for creating a collage of multiple screenshots. Moreover, Samsung A13 phones support multiple windows which can be handy when it comes to taking multiple screenshots quickly. To access multiple windows, press and hold the recent apps button and select two or more apps to be opened side by side.
