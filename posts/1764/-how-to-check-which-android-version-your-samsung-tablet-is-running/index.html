1. Identify Your Samsung Tablet Model#

  1. Swipe down from the top of your Samsung tablet’s screen to access the Notification Bar.
  2. Tap on the Settings icon to open the device’s settings.
  3. Locate the About Device menu and tap on it.
  4. Scroll down to the bottom of the page, where you should see the Android version that your tablet is running.
  5. You’ll also find the device’s model name and the serial number shown in the same box.
  6. To double-check the version, look for the number next to the version name in the same box.

2. Download & Install ‘Settings’ App On Your Tablet#

  1. Swipe down Notification bar and tap on Settings icon. The Settings icon is usually located on the home screen or Apps Menu.
  2. Once you click on the Settings icon, you should be taken to the main settings menu of your tablet.
  3. From the main settings menu, you will need to scroll down to find the About Device option.
  4. The About Device menu will provide you with information about your Samsung tablet, such as OS version, build number, etc.
  5. Scroll down to the bottom of the menu and you will see the Android version of your device.
  6. Different Android versions have different features and capabilities, so it’s important to know which version your device is running.

3. Open ‘Settings’ App & Tap On ‘About’#

  1. Swipe down the notification bar on your home screen. This brings up a list of options that is important for customizing your Samsung tablet.

2.Tap on the ‘Settings’ icon, this will open up the settings app for your device.

  1. Within the ‘Settings’ app, navigate to ‘About Device’ which is usually located near the bottom of the page in the list of options.

4.Scroll down the page until you find the ‘Android Version’. Here you can find the version of Android that your Samsung tablet is running.

5.The version number will be displayed right next to the ‘Android Version’ title. It will look something like ‘4.4.4.’

6.You can now check this number against the list of available Android versions to determine the exact version that your device is running.

4. Check Android Version on Your Samsung Tablet#

  1. Start by swiping your finger down from the top of the tablet’s screen to open the Notification Bar.
  2. Tap on the Settings icon which can be found on the Notification Bar.
  3. On the Settings menu, find the About Device option and tap it.
  4. Scroll down to the bottom of the About Device menu to locate the Android version.
  5. You will find the version number that is the Android version your Samsung tablet is running.
  6. The version number of the Android will usually have four digits which represent its major, minor, and security updates version such as 8.1.0.

Q: What is Android version? A: Android version is the version of the Android operating system that your Samsung tablet device is running. It is typically divided into three parts: Major version, minor version, and security patch.

Q: How can I check which Android version my Samsung tablet is running? A: To check which Android version your Samsung tablet is running, go to your device’s Settings > About Device > Android Version.

Q: What do the numbers in the Android version mean? A: The numbers in the Android version refer to major and minor versions (e.g. Android 10.0) and security patch (e.g. Android 10.0.1). The major version updates usually bring big changes, while the minor versions and security patches come with smaller improvements and bug fixes.

Q: How do I know if I have the latest version of Android? A: To check if your device is running the latest version of Android, go to Settings > System > Advanced > System Update. Here you will see the current version you are running and also available updates.

Q: What should I do if my device is not running the latest version of Android? A: If your device is not running the latest version of Android, you should update it. To update your Samsung tablet, go to Settings > System > Advanced > System Update and follow the instructions to install the update.
