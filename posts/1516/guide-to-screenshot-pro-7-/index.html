1. Overview of Microsoft Surface Pro 7#

Microsoft Surface Pro 7 is a 2-in-1 laptop/tablet hybrid computer released by Microsoft in October 2019. It comes with a 12.3-inch PixelSense Display with a resolution of 2736 x 1824 and a 10th-generation Intel Core processor. On the other hand, the Windows 10 operating system contains many features that make it easier and faster to work with. One of them is the ability to take screenshots. Taking screenshots on the Surface Pro 7 is quite simple and requires no additional software. All you need to do is press the Windows Key + Volume Down for around two seconds. In that time, the screenshot will be taken and saved in the Screenshots folder of the Pictures library. You can also capture a specific portion of your screen by pressing the Windows Key + Shift + S. This will open a screen cropping tool, allowing you to select what portion of the screen you want to capture. Finally, you can also use the Print Screen button located on the Type Cover to quickly take a screenshot of your entire screen. With these simple steps, you can now easily take screenshots on your Microsoft Surface Pro 7.

2. Setting up Screenshot Tool#

  1. The first step is to make sure the type cover for the Surface Pro 7 is connected and locked properly. This process is essential as it will ensure the keyboard shortcuts work correctly.

  2. To take a screenshot click the Windows key + Volume Down. This will be indicated by the screen flashing.

  3. Navigate to the desktop and open the Screenshots folder. This will be located in the Pictures folder on the computer.

  4. It’s important to note that screenshots taken on the Surface Pro 7 are saved as PNG files. The location and file name of the screenshot can be changed before saving.

  5. To edit the screenshot, simply open the image in the Paint 3D application. This will allow users to adjust the brightness, contrast, add text, etc.

  6. The final step is to share the screenshot. This can be done by right-clicking on the image and selecting the option to share. It will then be sent to the selected recipient.

3. How to Take a Screenshot#

Taking screenshots on Microsoft Surface Pro 7 has become a lot easier and more intuitive with its Windows 10 operating system. For you to take one, all you need to do is press the Windows Key and the Volume Down button at the same time. Hold it for a few seconds and a screenshot of your screen will be taken. This saved to a folder in your Pictures folder. You can also find these screenshots in the Recent folder in the Photos App.

Using the Snip & Sketch App, you can take and edit your screenshots any way you like. By opening the Snip & Sketch App, you will be able to select from Rectangular, Free form, and Full Screen snips. You can also delay the screenshot to give you enough time to switch to the window you want screenshot. Once you have taken a screenshot, the Snip & Sketch App will allow you to crop, add annotations and much more to your screenshot.

Once you are done, you can save your screenshot in .png, .gif or .jpeg format. You can also copy it o the clipboard so you can paste it on any other app or document. Furthermore, you can easily share these screenshots with other apps.

The Windows key plus the Print Screen button also allows you to take screenshots on your Surface Pro 7. This will create a screenshot of the entire screen and save it as a .png file in the Screenshot folder of the Pictures library.

On the other hand, you can also take screenshots on your Surface Pro 7 by using the Xbox App. By opening the Game bar with Windows Key + G, you can take a screenshot of the game that you are playing and view it in the Xbox App. Here you will be able to edit the screenshot with annotations as well.

Taking screenshots on Surface Pro 7 has never been easier. With the two methods mentioned, you can take quality screenshots and have them saved in only a few seconds of your time.

4. Tips for Taking Better Screenshots#

  1. To start, make sure you have the Surface Pro 7’s latest version of Windows 10 installed. To check, open the Start Menu and select Settings from the menu. Click on the Update & Security option.
  2. Once you’ve confirmed your Windows 10 version, you are ready to take a screenshot. To do this, you’ll use the default Windows 10 feature Snip & Sketch.
  3. To take a screenshot, press the Windows Key + Volume Down buttons at the same time. Hold for two seconds. This will open the Snip & Sketch app and the screenshot will be taken.
  4. To edit the screenshot, simply open the Snip & Sketch app. This app allows you to crop, draw, or add text to the screenshot.
  5. To share the screenshot, simply select the ‘Share’ icon from the toolbar at the top of the Snip & Sketch window. This will open a new window with options to share the screenshot to an app or to your clipboard.
  6. Once you are finished, click the ‘Done’ button in the Snip & Sketch app to save the screenshot to your Pictures folder.
