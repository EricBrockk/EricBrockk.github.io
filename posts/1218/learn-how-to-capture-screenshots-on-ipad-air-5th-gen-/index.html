2. Identify the Different Types of Screenshots You Can Capture#

Capturing a screenshot is a function found on all devices. As an iPad Air (5th Gen) user, you can capture screenshots your device in several ways. Before you capture a screenshot, first identify the different types of screenshots you can capture.

The most common type of screenshot is the full-screen screenshot. To take a full-screen screenshot, press and hold the Sleep/Wake button located at the top of the device. Simultaneously press the Home button located at the bottom of the device. The screen will flash and the screenshot will be saved in the Photos app.

Another type of screenshot is the window screenshot. This is a screenshot of the active window on your device. To take a window screenshot, press and hold the Command and Shift buttons and press the number 4 key. A crosshair icon will appear. When you spot the window you want to capture, press and hold the mouse click button, and drag your cursor over the window. Release the mouse click button and the screenshot of the window will be saved in the Photos app.

Finally, you can also capture a screenshot of a specific area of the screen. To take a screenshot of a specific area, press and hold the Command and Shift buttons and press the number 4 key. A crosshair icon will appear. When you spot the specific area of the screen you want to capture, press and hold the mouse click button, and drag your cursor over the area. Release the mouse click button and the screenshot of the area will be saved in the Photos app.

In addition to taking screenshots, you can also record videos of the screen. To take a screen recording, you can open the QuickTime Player app. Click the File menu at the top of the screen and select the New Screen Recording option. Click the arrow next to the record button, and select the iPad Air (5th Gen) option from the drop-down list.

3. Learn About the Different Ways to Take a Screenshot#

Taking screenshots on an iPad Air (5th Gen) is a quick and easy process. The simplest way is to press and hold the Sleep/Wake button and simultaneously press the Home button. This method will capture whatever is currently being displayed on the iPad’s screen. To get an image of an open app on the iPad, the user should first open the desired app before taking the screenshot. After the screenshot is taken, it will be saved to the device’s Photo Albums, where it can be shared, edited, and saved for later.

An alternate, yet more complex, method of taking screenshots on the iPad Air is to use the iPad’s Command Center. To do this, the user must first open the Command Center by swiping up from the bottom edge of the screen. Next, the user must tap the Screenshot icon to capture the currently displayed app. As with the simpler method, the screenshot will be saved to the device’s Photo Albums.

For screenshots taken with either method, the user can then edit and share them directly from the iPad Air. This can be done with Apple’s built-in editing tools, which include crop, filters, rotate, and markup tools. After the screenshot is edited, users can either save a copy to their library or post it directly to social media sites such as Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram.

The iPad Air is also capable of taking screenshots of multiple-page documents, such as magazines or books. To do this, the user should select Page Screenshot from the Command Center menu. After selecting this option, the user will be able to take a screenshot of the entire document, rather than having to capture each page one at a time. Once the screenshot is taken, it will be saved to the iPad’s Photos Albums, where it can then be edited and shared.

Finally, the iPad Air is also capable of taking screenshots of individual sections of an app or webpage. To do this, the user should select Screen Recording from the Command Center menu. This option allows the user to choose a section of the iPhone’s screen to capture by dragging a rectangle over the desired area. Once the selection is complete, the user can save the screenshot or share it directly online.
