Are you wanting to switch from an iPhone to an Android device but don’t want to leave behind your contacts? Well, there are several ways to easily transfer contacts from iPhone to Android without having to rely on iCloud.

Using a SIM card A SIM card or Subscriber Identity Module, is the best way to transfer contacts from an iOS device to an Android device. On an iPhone, the contacts are usually stored on the SIM card and not within the device itself.

To begin, you will need to take out the SIM card from you iPhone and insert it in you Android device. Then follow the steps below:

  1. On your Android device, open the Contacts app.
  2. Tap the menu icon in the upper right corner and select “Import/Export”.
  3. Select the option to “Import from SIM card”.
  4. You can choose to import all contacts or just selected contacts.

Once the process is complete, your contacts from the iPhone will be available in the Contacts app on your Android device.

Using an Email App If you don’t want to use a SIM card, you can also use an email app to transfer your contacts from your iPhone to your Android device.

  1. On you iPhone, open the Contacts app.
  2. Select all the contacts you want to transfer and tap “Share Contact”.
  3. Select “Mail”.
  4. In the “To” field, put in the email address associated with your Android device.
  5. Tap “Send”.

Now, open the email app in your Android device and you should see all the contacts you sent from your iPhone. Open each contact one by one and tap “Add to contacts”.

Using Google Drive You can also use Google Drive to transfer contacts from your iPhone to your Android device.

  1. On your iPhone, open the Contacts app.
  2. Tap the menu icon in the upper left corner and select “Import/Export”.
  3. Select “Export to Google CSV”.
  4. Go to Google Drive on your Android device and tap “+”.
  5. Select “Upload” and choose the file you just exported from your iPhone.

Once the file is uploaded, open it and your contacts will be available on your Android device.
