Are you struggling to find a way to transfer your contacts from your iPhone to your new Android device without the use of iCloud? Look no further! This step-by-step guide will show you the way to make your transition from iPhone to Android seamless. Are you making the switch from an iPhone to an Android device? That’s great! You’ll love the new possibilities that come with Android. But before getting started, you need to know how to safely transfer all your contacts to the new device. Transferring contacts from iPhone to Android without iCloud is an easy process. This step by step guide will provide you with the instructions you need to complete the transfer quickly and easily.

  1. First, make sure that you have enabled iCloud’s “Contacts” sync option on your iPhone. To do this, go to Settings > iCloud > Contacts and make sure it’s set to “On”. This will allow you to synchronize contacts between your iPhone and iCloud.

  2. Next, you’ll need to export your contacts from iCloud. To do this, open the iCloud website and log in with your Apple ID. Then, go to “Contacts” and click the gear icon in the lower-left corner. Select “Export vCard” from the menu. This will download your contacts to your computer in the form of a .vcf file.

  3. Finally, you’ll need to import the contacts into your Android phone. To do this, open the Contacts app and select the “Import Contacts” option. Then, select the .vcf file you exported from iCloud and your contacts will be transferred to your Android phone.

Steps for iPhone Android Transfer

Transferring Contacts from iPhone to Android without iCloud

  1. Prepare Your Devices: On your iPhone, go to Settings > Your Name > iCloud and turn off Contacts. On your Android phone, ensure that your Google account is set up.

  2. Export Your Contacts from iPhone: On your iPhone, open the Contacts app, tap Groups in the top left corner, and select All iCloud. Tap “Select All” in the top right corner, to select all of your contacts and then tap the Share icon in the bottom left corner. Select “Mail” and enter your own email address.

  3. Import Contacts to Gmail: On your computer, open your email and download the vcard file which contains all of your contacts. Log into your Gmail account and go to Contacts. Click “Import” from the More drop down menu and select the downloaded vcard file.

  4. Transfer Contacts from Gmail to Android: On your Android phone, open the Contacts app and go to the “Settings” tab. Tap “Accounts” and select “Google”. Log into your account and select “Contacts” to sync. This will transfer all of your contacts from your Gmail account to your device.

1. Overview of the steps needed for contact transfer#

First, it is important to disable sync contacts on the settings of IPhone. This can be done by opening the settings application and finding the iCloud tab. Here, you should toggle off the switch beside contacts. After, you should sign in your Google account on the Android phone - this will be the destination of the contacts. To export contacts from the iPhone, open the contacts app and select ‘All iCloud’. You should then click ‘Select All’ to choose all the contacts. Finally, you can choose the ‘Share’ option and choose the method of transfer such as Email or AirDrop. After these steps, all of the contacts should now be present on the Android phone.

2. Preparation for contact transfer from iPhone to Android#

  1. Firstly, unlock both iPhone and Android device, and make sure they are connected to Wi-fi or mobile data connection.
  2. In the settings of the iPhone, locate the option to disable contacts then switch it off.
  3. On the Android device, sign in to your Google account so that the contacts can be automatically synced.
  4. Open the Contacts app on the iPhone and select ‘All iCloud’ to reveal a list of contacts.
  5. Next, choose the ‘Select All’ option from the top menu to choose all listed contacts.
  6. Finally, tap on the Share button and choose ‘Move to Android’ option to start transferring contacts from iPhone to Android.

3. Exploring different software options available#

After disabling contacts in settings in iPhone and signing in with your Google account into your Android phone, it is time to export the contacts from your iPhone. Firstly, access the Contacts app in iPhone, click “All iCloud” at the top of the screen and select “Select All”. Then, select “Share Contact” at the bottom of the screen and choose “Mail”. You will be prompted to enter your own email address and click “Send”. Finally, check your email account and open the email which you have just sent. Copy all of the contacts’ information in the email and paste it into the vCard file in Notepad. Save the file as “vcf” format, sign in your Google account in your Android phone and upload the vcf file. This will transfer all of your contacts to your Android phone without iCloud.

4. Executing the contact transfer from iPhone to Android#

Next, press the Share button and tap Mail, type in an email address then tap Send. After that, open the same mail address in your Android phone. Finally, open the mail and press Import Contacts from the menu. Your contacts have been transferred to your Android phone. To ensure that the transfer is completed, you can check if your contacts are visible in the Contacts app in your Android phone.

Q1: How do I transfer contacts from my iPhone to my Android without using iCloud? A1: To transfer contacts from an iPhone to an Android without using iCloud, you will need to use a third-party app such as Google Drive, iCloud Drive, Shareit, or Copy My Data. With any of these apps, you can easily transfer your contacts from iPhone to Android without iCloud.

Q2: What steps do I need to take in order to use one of the apps listed above? A2: First, open the app on both your iPhone and your Android device. On your iPhone, select the contacts you wish to transfer. On your Android device, click the ‘Import’ button. Finally, follow any additional instructions on your app to complete the transfer.

Q3: Can I transfer contacts over WiFi? A3: Yes, with the right app you can transfer contacts from your iPhone to Android over WiFi. Apps like Google Drive, Shareit, and Copy My Data all have the option to share data over WiFi.

Q4: Does transferring contacts with an app cost money? A4: No, most of the apps listed above for transferring contacts are free to download and use.

Q5: Will my current contact list be replaced by the import? A5: No, the imported contacts will be added to your current contact list. You can delete any contacts you don’t want to keep.
