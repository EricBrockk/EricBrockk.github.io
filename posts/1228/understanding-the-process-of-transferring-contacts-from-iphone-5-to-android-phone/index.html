1. Overview of transferring contacts from iPhone 5 to Android Phone#

After you launch the Move to iOS app on your Android device and agree to the terms and conditions, you will be prompted to create a secure connection between your Android device and the iPhone 5. To create the connection, you will need to enter a code on your Android device that will be displayed on your iPhone 5. Make sure to provide the same Apple ID and Password that is associated with the iPhone 5.

Once the connection has been established, the Move to iOS app will begin transferring your contacts from the iPhone 5 over to your Android device. During this process, the app will recognize all of your contacts separately, so you are able to select which ones you want to transfer. Additionally, Move to iOS app will also transfer any additional data such as photos, videos, and music.

In addition to using the Move to iOS app, you can also transfer your contacts manually. To do so, you will need to first export your iPhone 5 contacts as a .vcf file - this can usually be found in the contacts section of your Settings app on the iPhone 5. Once you have exported the .vcf file, you will then need to import the file to your Android device. The exact steps for this process can vary depending on the type of Android phone you are using, but the general steps consist of importing the file into the Contacts section of your phone.

It is important to remember that when you transfer contacts from an iPhone 5 to an Android phone, the contacts can only be synced one way. This means any changes that you make to the contacts on the Android phone will not be reflected on the iPhone 5, and vice-versa.

Finally, when you are done transferring your contacts, be sure to delete the Move to iOS app from your Android device to ensure that everything has been safely copied over. Additionally, you should also double-check your contacts list to make sure that all of your contacts have been successfully moved over.

2. Choosing the right method for the transfer#

  1. It is important to know what type of Android phone you are using before you begin the contact transfer process. Different phones have different capabilities and you should be sure that there is a compatible method for the transfer.
  2. When transferring contacts from an iPhone 5 to an Android device, you can either use the Google account or a third-party app like Move to iOS.
  3. With Google account, you can sign in to your Google account on both phones. On the iPhone, go to Settings > Contacts > Accounts and tap “Add Account”. Select Google and enter your account information. Then, on the Android phone, go to Settings > Accounts > Add Account and select Google. Enter your Google account information to link the phones.
  4. Once they are connected, open up the Contacts app and sync them, so your contacts are transferred to your Android phone.
  5. The other option is to install the Move to iOS app from the Google Play Store. Open Settings on your iPhone and tap the “Move Data from Android” option. Follow the on-screen instructions and enter the one-time code provided by the app. The contacts will be transferred to your Android phone in a few minutes.
  6. You can also transfer contacts from your iPhone to your computer before transferring them to your Android phone. To do this, connect your iPhone to your computer and use an application like iTunes or iCloud to export the contacts into a vCard or CSV file. Then, you can import those files to your Android phone.

3. Transferring contacts using iCloud#

  1. First, make sure that you have an iCloud account to which you can back up your contacts from the iPhone 5. You can do this by opening the Settings app on your phone and selecting the iCloud tab.
  2. Once registered, open the iCloud tab in the Settings app and select Contacts. All the contacts stored on your iPhone 5 should appear here.
  3. Then, open the Move to iOS app from the Google Play Store on your Android phone and tap “Continue” to start the transfer.
  4. Select the “Transfer from Android” option on your iPhone 5, and then select “Move Data from Android”. Enter the code that appears on the Android phone to begin the transfer process.
  5. On your Android device, select the “Contacts” option and tap on “Finish” when the transfer is complete.
  6. Finally, check the contacts on your Android phone to ensure that all of the contacts from your iPhone 5 have been successfully transferred.

4. Transferring contacts using third-party apps#

First, ensure that both your iPhone 5 and Android phone are connected to a good WiFi connection. Once you have verified this, use the Google Play Store to download the Move to iOS app. After you have downloaded the app, open it on your Android phone. The app will then search for devices that are near it.

When your iPhone 5 is found, tap it in the list of options and enter the code that will appear on your Android phone’s screen. After that, select the content you wish to transfer from your iPhone 5, such as contacts, photos, and videos, and click the Continue button.

The app will do the transferring process for you. If you are transferring contacts, the app will check whether there are any contacts in your iPhone 5 that are not in your Android phone. If there are, it will add those contacts to your Android phone.

Once the transferring process is complete, make sure to check on your Android phone if all of the contacts have been successfully transferred. If you have any duplicate contacts, you can delete them from your Android phone. Additionally, make sure that all the contacts you transferred are in the correct format.

Finally, if you need to transfer more content from your iPhone 5 to your Android phone, you can always do it again by repeating the same steps. To delete the Move to iOS app from your Android phone, simply open the app, and tap the Uninstall button.
